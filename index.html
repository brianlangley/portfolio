About me

Hello there! I'm Brian, a passionate 2nd-year software development student at the Bit Academy in Amsterdam.
With an international upbringing that spans multiple countries including Indonesia, Singapore, Hong Kong, South Africa, and the UAE. I have been fortunate enough to have had the pleasure of immersing and embracing myself within several diverse cultures, incorporating their values into my own personal and professional life.

Front-end development captures my heart, combining creativity with problem-solving abilities. I am passionate about creating visually stunning and user-friendly web applications with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript. I am competent in modern frameworks such as React and Vue.js, also leveraging CSS frameworks such as Bootstrap and Tailwind CSS for seamless, responsive, and intuitive designs.

With a keen eye for design and a knack for wire-framing and prototyping using tools such as Figma, I bring an emphasis on user foucued experiences to every project. By creating mockups, I provide my clients with a visual representation of the product. This approach allows me to gather useful and informative feedback, which I can then incorporate and build upon to refine and improve the overall user journey.

Moreover, I have experience working with Scrum and Agile methodologies, promoting effective teamwork, adaptability, and iterative development to deliver successful projects.

Beyond technical skills, I pride myself on my strong communication and teamwork abilities, enabling effective collaboration with colleagues and clients. I believe that my problem-solving mindset and attention to detail allows me to tackle unique situations and deliver high-quality results. I'm always open and passionate about learning new technologies, and expanding my skill set to stay at the forefront of the rapidly evolving world of software development.

Projects

Project: Wanderlust

Inspired by platforms like Pinterest, Wanderlust is a photo liking application developed in vanilla JavaScript. It provides users with a visual journey through a collection of stunning photographs. Where they can browse through a diverse range of images, like their favorite photos, and store them as personal favorites.

BitGreen: The Green Coin

BitGreen is the front-end implementation of a fictional cryptocurrency website promoting eco-friendly initiatives. It consists of two sites developed using Bootstrap and Tailwind CSS, showcasing visually appealing and responsive designs.

Lambiek: Comiclopedia v2.0

Undertaken by a group of enthusiastic students from the Bit Academy. The team embarked on a mission to revitalise and enhance the existing Lambiek.net Comic Wikipedia, also known as the Comiclopedia. Creating a new and improved version of the Comiclopedia that incorporates modern technologies and innovative features to provide an even more comprehensive and engaging experience for users.
